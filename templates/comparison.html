Historical Election Influence

Here, you can select between two elections and compare the differences in their results. On the right hand side of the page, we will display positive numbers when Election 1 had greater results and negative numbers when Election 2 had greater results.

Use this data in conjunction with the analysis of the previous page to see how elections compare given their voter influences. By looking at the default example of 2012 and 1988, we can see that voter turnout was six percentage points higher and the GDP was more than $14,000 higher for the 2012 election. Are people more motivated to vote when the country is thriving economically?
